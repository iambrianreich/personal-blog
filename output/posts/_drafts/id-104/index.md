Sarah Drasner is one of those developers whose work consistently leaves me either inspired or paralyzed with imposter syndrome. There's no between.

And now, I find out, Sarah is also a brilliant manager.

I'll just say it plainly: [Engineering Management for the Rest of Us](https://amzn.to/3u53AjX) is a masterpiece. Anyone in engineering management or considering travelling the management path stands to benefit from Sarah Drasner's experience at places like Netlify, Microsoft, and Google.

I became a manager about a year and a half ago. In a small company line mine, I guess it's just assumed that a productive Individual Contributor (geek speak for _a person that does tasks and doesn't manage_) will also excel at management. You're not told what makes a good manager. You're not told what a manager does. You just... sort of... _manage_.

I got into management armed only with a sense of commitment to doing good work, genuine care for the people on my team, and a few examples from my career of folks I felt were good and not so good managers. I sort of knew the kind of manager I wanted to be.
